General principles and goals of information security

Principles of designing secure systems

  • Least privilege
  • Fail-safe defaults
  • Economy of mechanism (simplicity)
  • Complete mediation (access control)
  • Security should not rely on secrecy of design (open design principle)
  • Compartmentalization
  • Usability
  • Defense in depth
  • Question assumptions

Goals of information security

Protecting data and services

  • Confidentiality
  • Integrity
  • Availability
  • Non-repudiation

Effective defense

  • Deterrence
  • Prevention
  • Detection
  • Recovery
  • Investigation
